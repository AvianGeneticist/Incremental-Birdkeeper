Turkswine are large, stout birds with thick, hooked beaks and large, sensitive nostrils. They're commonly used to find all manner of resources.
Pedogeons - named for their eternal youth - are an uncommon sight in cities, where they attempt to trick normal pigeons into lactating for them.
Nestpeckers are excitable cousins of the woodpecker family, adept at using tools to carry things they find. Their excellent communication skills means they can initiate foraging operations indepedently.

Basic Egg Producers
Sulphur Hens are common and docile, as well as easily noticed thanks to their bright yellow coats. Their feather and egg colour comes from a coat of sulphurous compounds that repulses most predators.
Sulphur Roosters are docile compared to most Roosters, though they still have some bite for those who get too close. Sulphur Roosters are typically less brightly coloured than hens, and do not directly lay eggs.


Buttewrens are tiny and beautiful, with their black-feathered bodies coming into beautiful, multicoloured, stained-glass-like wings. They are very common and sought by collectors.
Moth-winged, hummingbird-like avians that average 2 feet long with 3 foot wingspans. Albamoths have mutualistic relationship with Buttewrens; Albamoths watch over Buttewren nests and protect them from predators, in exchange for excess food gathered by the nesting Buttewrens.


Bruthrushes are common verdant birds found in temperate regions. Their large, leafy wings allow them to blend into foliage-rich environments, making them tricky to spot at first glance.


Special Egg Producers
Swarm Ducks are social waterfowl that congregate in enormous swarms for laying season. Their eggs get higher in quality the more there are in the swarm.


Egg Multipliers
Cragowaries are fierce birds with rocklike skin and pointy feathers. They are exceptionally aggressive, attacking anything that could be deemed as a threat to them.


Basic Milk Producers
Dromingos are sluggish, calm birds that are typically used to produce milk for other bird species. The reddish substance is known to fetch a high price in poorer regions.


Udder Hens are genetically modified leghorn chickens that have udders sprouting between their legs. Their eggs are typically unsuitable for hatching, however their milk is a widely consumed commodity.


Unique Producers
Prokareagles are relatively common microorganismic birds that often rapidly multiply once they are set in a region. It takes them a long time to produce any worthwhile amount of egg mass.


Unit Producers
When engorged in nutrients, a Sulphur Hen with grow to thrice its normal size, becoming a Sulphur Matriarch. Matriarchs do not produce eggs; instead, they automatically hatch and raise Sulphur Hens.


Verdant Boosters
Apiform Hummingbirds are easily noticed thanks to their yellow and black striped appearance. This gives them a beelike appearance, even though they are incredibly docile and do not have stingers.


Support
Large, jolly chickens that exude a calming warmth that encourages egg development around them. Tepids have very tight social structures: only 30 of them can exist on the same farm.
